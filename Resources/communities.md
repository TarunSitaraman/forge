# Communities

*Communities (forums, Discord/Slack groups, mailing lists) worth
actually participating in — not just lurking in. A community earns a
place here for what you've gotten out of engaging with it, since the
value of a community is almost entirely in participation quality, which
you can only assess by actually being active in it.*

---

## Admission criteria

Add a community only after participating for a while (asking or
answering something, not just reading) and can state:
- What specifically you got from it — a fast, high-quality answer to a
  hard question; access to people building the same thing; a pipeline to
  opportunities.
- Its actual activity level and signal-to-noise ratio, honestly — a
  community that's gone quiet or turned mostly promotional has stopped
  earning its place even if it once did.

## Maintenance

Communities change character over time — faster than blogs or books.
Review this list periodically: has a community you once valued gone
quiet, gotten noisy, or shifted focus? Prune accordingly rather than
letting this page reflect a stale snapshot of where you used to hang out.
