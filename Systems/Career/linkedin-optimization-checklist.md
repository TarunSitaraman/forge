# LinkedIn Optimization Checklist

*Guidance: LinkedIn is a search-and-skim surface, not a resume clone —
recruiters search by keyword and skim in seconds. Optimize for both.
Check each item against your actual live profile.*

---

## Headline

- [ ] States what you do + your specialization, not just a job title
      copy-pasted ("Backend Engineer | Distributed Systems, Go, AWS" —
      not just "Software Engineer at Company")
- [ ] Includes keywords a recruiter would actually search for in your
      target role

## About section

- [ ] Opens with a specific, concrete line (not "Passionate software
      engineer with X years of experience...")
- [ ] States what you're looking for, if actively job-searching —
      recruiters filter on this
- [ ] Under ~4 short paragraphs; this is skimmed, not read closely

## Experience entries

- [ ] Each role has 2-4 bullets, impact-first (reuse the audit from
      `resume-audit-checklist.md` — the same bar applies here)
- [ ] Current role is marked current and up to date
- [ ] Media/links attached where relevant (a demo, a repo, a writeup) —
      profiles with attached media get more engagement

## Skills section

- [ ] Top 3 pinned skills match what you actually want to be found for
      — these are weighted most heavily in recruiter search
- [ ] No skill listed that you couldn't defend being asked about

## Featured section

- [ ] At least one real artifact featured (project, article, talk) — an
      empty featured section is a missed, free signal

## Activity / visibility

- [ ] Profile photo is professional and recent
- [ ] Custom URL set (`linkedin.com/in/yourname`, not the default
      numeric one)
- [ ] Open-to-work signal set correctly for your actual situation
      (public vs. recruiters-only — know which you've chosen)

## Before you consider this done

- [ ] Search your own target job title on LinkedIn and check: does your
      profile surface for the keywords a recruiter would actually use?
- [ ] Ask one person outside your field to skim your profile for 10
      seconds and say back what they think you do — if it's wrong, the
      headline/about needs work.
