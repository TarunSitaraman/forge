# Forge Engineering Constitution

## Mission
Forge exists to turn engineering practice into a durable, searchable, and connected knowledge system.

## Philosophy
Knowledge should be canonical, reusable, and linked. Notes should help future work happen faster without hiding uncertainty.

## Design Principles
- One concept has one canonical home.
- Pages stay focused and link outward.
- Templates protect consistency.
- Indexes improve navigation without duplicating content.

## Canonical Knowledge Rules
Create new pages only after searching for an existing source. Merge overlapping ideas into the strongest canonical page.

## AI Responsibilities
AI contributions must follow standards, preserve links, avoid duplication, and leave maintainable artifacts.

## Human Responsibilities
Humans decide priorities, validate correctness, and retire obsolete knowledge.

## Documentation Lifecycle
Draft, review, stabilize, update, and archive when replaced.

## Review Standards
Every page must satisfy [[Validation Checklist]] before becoming stable.

## Scalability Goals
Forge should remain maintainable for at least ten years by preferring small connected pages over large duplicated documents.

## Anti-Patterns To Avoid
- Duplicate canonical pages
- Unlinked notes
- Overlong explanations in cheat sheets
- Missing metadata
- New folders without architecture updates
