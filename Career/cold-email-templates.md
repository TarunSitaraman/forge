# Cold Email Templates

*Guidance: these are starting structures to adapt, not scripts to send
verbatim — a cold email that reads as copy-pasted is worse than none. Fill
in every bracket with something specific to the actual recipient.*

---

## Template: Informational / advice request

```
Subject: Quick question about {specific, specific topic — not "career advice"}

Hi {name},

{One specific, genuine reason you're reaching out to THEM — a project
they worked on, a talk they gave, a specific path they took. Not "I
found your profile impressive."}

I'm {one line: who you are + what you're working toward}. I had a
specific question: {the actual question — specific enough that a
30-second answer would genuinely help, not "any advice?"}

If you have 15 minutes sometime in the next couple weeks, I'd value a
short call — but even a quick reply to the question above would help a
lot. No worries if you're not able to.

Thanks,
{name}
```

## Template: Referral / internal contact request

```
Subject: {Role} at {Company} — quick referral question

Hi {name},

{Specific connection point — mutual contact, shared background, a
project of theirs relevant to the role.}

I just applied for {specific role, link if public} at {Company} and
noticed you work there. I know referral requests from near-strangers can
be a lot to ask — no pressure at all, but if you've had a good experience
there and would be willing to refer me, I'd really appreciate it. Happy
to send my resume or a quick summary of my background either way.

Thanks for considering it,
{name}
```

## Template: Cold outreach after an event/meeting

```
Subject: Following up from {event name}

Hi {name},

Great {talking with you / hearing your talk} at {event} about {specific
topic discussed}. {One specific thing they said that you're following up
on.}

{The specific ask, if any — or just "wanted to stay in touch" if this is
relationship-building rather than an ask.}

{name}
```

---

## Rules that apply to all of these
- One specific, genuine reason for reaching out to THIS person — a
  template filled in generically reads as generic no matter how well
  written the template is.
- One clear, small ask — multiple asks in a cold email reduce response
  rate.
- Make it easy to say no (explicit "no worries if not") — this
  paradoxically increases response rate by lowering the cost of replying.
- Never send the same exact wording to more than a couple of people —
  vary it enough that it doesn't read as a mail-merge if compared.
