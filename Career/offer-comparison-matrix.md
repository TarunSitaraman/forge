point is to make the tradeoffs explicit instead of deciding on vibes or
whichever number is biggest.*

## Best practices
- Annualize equity realistically (using a real valuation/strike price
  estimate, not the offer letter's optimistic headline number) — sticker
  equity value is the most commonly overweighted number in comp
  comparisons.
- Include your current situation as a real baseline column, not just an
  implicit assumption — "staying" is a genuine option to weigh, not a
  null choice.
- Don't skip the qualitative rows (team, growth, stability) just because
  they're harder to quantify — they're frequently the actual driver of
  job satisfaction a year later.
