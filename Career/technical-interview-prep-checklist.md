# Technical Interview Prep Checklist

*Guidance: a checklist for the run-up to a specific technical interview
— not a general "how to study CS" plan. Fill in the specifics for the
actual interview you have scheduled.*

---

## 1-2 weeks before

- [ ] Confirmed interview format (coding / system design / take-home /
      pairing) and, if possible, what topics are typically covered for
      this specific company/role
- [ ] Ran at least one full mock session per format using
      `Technologies/Prompt-Library/Interview/technical-interview-mock-session.md`
      or `Technologies/Prompt-Library/System-Design/system-design-interview-style.md`
- [ ] Reviewed `Technologies/Competitive-Programming/pattern-tracker.md` for
      any weak pattern likely to come up, and did targeted reps via
      `Technologies/Prompt-Library/Competitive-Programming/cp-similar-problem-generator.md`
- [ ] Prepared 2-3 STAR stories in case of a hybrid behavioral+technical
      format (see `star-story-bank.md`)

## 2-3 days before

- [ ] Re-ran a mock session, incorporating feedback from the first one
- [ ] Reviewed `Technologies/Competitive-Programming/complexity-cheatsheet.md`
      if the format includes live coding
- [ ] Prepared 2-3 thoughtful questions to ask the interviewer — specific
      to the team/role, not generic ("what's the tech stack")

## Day before

- [ ] Confirmed logistics: time zone, link/location, what to bring
      (laptop, ID), and tested any required software/IDE setup
- [ ] Light review only — re-read STAR stories and pattern tracker, no
      new material
- [ ] Planned environment: quiet space, good internet, water nearby

## Day of

- [ ] Arrived/logged in 5-10 minutes early
- [ ] Have a notepad/scratch space ready for system design or math
- [ ] Reminder: narrate your thinking out loud, ask clarifying questions
      before diving into code, and state assumptions explicitly

## After the interview

- [ ] Logged the experience: what was asked, what went well, what to
      improve — feed into `Technologies/Competitive-Programming/contest-reflection.md`
      style reflection or a personal notes entry
- [ ] Sent a brief thank-you note if appropriate for the company culture
